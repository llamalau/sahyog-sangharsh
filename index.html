The Challenge

Sahyog is a non-profit community organization in Mumbai that works for sustainable change in women and children from marginalized communities. Their Sangharsh program focuses on early identification and intervention for children with developmental disabilities — including intellectual disability, autism, cerebral palsy, and hearing impairment.

Special Educators (SEs) already use WhatsApp to send education plans to parents and collect video/text evidence of compliance. But the process is manual, scattered across individual chats, and lacks systematic tracking against the NIEPID Disha assessment framework.

This storyboard walks through a purpose-built dashboard that orchestrates the entire SE-to-caregiver workflow: from student assessment, to weekly task planning, to WhatsApp delivery, to evidence collection and progress tracking.

A Vision for Scale

What starts with Gulshan and Aliza can reach hundreds of families. WhatsApp is already the communication tool of choice in India's marginalized communities — the caregivers don't need to download a new app or learn a new interface. They just receive messages, watch videos, and send back evidence.

The dashboard gives Special Educators the structure they need: systematic planning using NIEPID Disha benchmarks, bilingual delivery, automated reminders, and progress tracking that makes invisible work visible. Every video submission is a data point. Every feedback message is a connection.

From one community center in Mumbai to every marginalized family with a smartphone — education delivered through the tool they already trust.
